What is Domestic Stalking?

Stalking is criminal activity consisting of repeated and persistent attempts to impose unwanted communication and/or contact on another person, including the repeated following and harassing of another person. For example, sending flowers, writing love notes, and waiting for someone outside her place of work are actions that, on their own, are not criminal. When these actions are coupled with an intent to instill fear, to intimidate or cause injury, however, they may constitute a pattern of behaviour that is illegal. Stalking is also a behaviour associated with domestic violence, where one partner attempts to dominate and control the other.

Domestic stalking is being stalked by a family member or a person you are, or have been, in an intimate relationship with.

Introducing AVA



Anti-Violence App (AVA)

The Anti-Violence App (AVA) designed by Brisbane startup SkillzApps, is an application designed to aid victims of domestic stalking and violence. AVA is presented in a mobile form, with consolidated resources for improving and protecting the safety of victims. AVA has a low barrier of entry and is designed to be easy for users to access and use.

AVA is a comprehensive tool that has native interfaces for all relevant tasks, which allows users to keep most of their activity confined within the space of the AVA app. This helps to protect the user, especially in making sure that prying eyes are unable to see sensitive content such as recorded evidence.

Setting AVA apart from other existing solutions is the fact that it takes care to ensure that the content within the app is presented in a concise and easy to comprehend style. AVA is designed to be comprehensive and fully-featured, making sure to include a more complete set of functions compared to existing solutions.


Our Audience

AVA will focus on helping the victims of domestic stalking, domestic violence, intimidation, as well as assist emergency services in responding to instances of violence as they occur.

Primary Features



App Overview

The AVA app contains 4 key areas of use, each designed to assist the user in a specific way. The four key areas of use are split into two main categories of use, support-based and emergency-use.

General Information and Support Tool are more information heavy than the Emergency Response Tool and Evidence Diary, and falls under the ‘support-based’ category of use. Emergency Response Tool and Evidence Diary are tools that fall under ‘emergency use’ as they are meant to assist users in situations of emergency or danger so that they can better protect themselves.


General Information

The intended aim of this segment is to provide basic-level guidance and information to users by supplying them with definitions of domestic violence and stalking. Icons representing specific forms of abuse are also included to help users better understand the various kinds of behaviours that are also considered ‘abusive’, apart from the commonly known ‘physical abuse’.


Emergency Response Tool

Mainly for use when victims need to contact emergency services or someone from the emergency contact database quickly in situations of danger. This tool has a contact database of phone numbers, which allows the user to store the numbers of established emergency services such as the police, refuge helpline and violence counselling, as well as personal emergency contacts. In this tool, there will be a number of emergency services speed dials that are displayed to the user upon entry so that users do not have to go through the contact database when there is an urgent situation at hand.


Support Tool

Mainly contains information meant for assisting victims, such as safety planning and legal advice. The safety plans are in place to help keep the victims and their loved ones safe, and comes in the form of customisable checklists. There is a selection of safety plans, each targeting a specific situation so the user can be better equipped to deal with a variety of scenarios. In partnership with the Women’s Legal Services and their Penda app, the Support Tool includes legal information in a Q&A format. The Support Tool also has a feature showing where the nearest support services are location. These services are targeted based on the user’s current location and shows additional information such as opening hours, address etc.


Evidence Diary

This feature has two major components, the diary archive and the recording features. Although this tool can be used in both emergency and non-emergency scenarios, it is designed to let users record evidence should they be involved in a situation of concern. The archive is there so that users have a point where all their evidence entries are stored. These entries can be saved and backed up to the cloud where they can later be accessed through the AVA web interface on any device. The Evidence Diary allows for three different forms of recording evidence, namely written, audio and photo/video. These three formats were selected as different methods of recording evidence suit different situations of abuse, and thus having a selection of formats makes collecting evidence easier for the user.


Added Features



Security - Concealment Settings

Considering the sensitive nature of AVA, as well as the kind of content that is stored inside the app, security is important as it concerns data security and privacy. Should the data stored on AVA be compromised, the personal safety and wellbeing of the user could be affected. Therefore, the feature for concealing the app has been included.
